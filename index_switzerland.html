Switzerland
It is a federal republic composed of 26 cantons.

Switzerland, officially the Swiss Confederation, is a country situated in the confluence of Western,  Central, and Southern Europe.

Etymology
The English name Switzerland is a compound containing Switzer.

The name Switzer is from the Alemannic Schwiizer, in origin an inhabitant of Schwyz and its associated territory, one of the Waldstätte cantons which formed the nucleus of the Old Swiss Confederacy.

History
Have been dated to around 5300 BC.

The earliest known cultural tribes of the area were members of the Hallstatt and La Tène cultures, named after the archaeological site of La Tène on the north side of Lake Neuchâtel.

Understand More

Politics
A new Swiss Constitution was adopted in 1999.

There are three main governing bodies on the federal level: the bicameral parliament (legislative), the Federal Council (executive) and the Federal Court (judicial).

Science
Resulting in a nearly CO2-free electricity-generating network.

The most dense rail network in Europe of 5,250 kilometres (3,260 mi) carries over 596 million passengers annually (as of 2015).

Environment
The many delicate valleys separated by high mountains.

The mountainous regions themselves are also vulnerable, with a rich range of plants not found at other altitudes, and experience some pressure from visitors and grazing.
